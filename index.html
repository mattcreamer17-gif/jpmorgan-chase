A read on where AI fits inside JPMorganChase's stack, and where it does not

You passed 230,000 LLM Suite users and Dimon put AI at the top of the 2025 letter, which means the MRM team is now validating generative outputs on an eight-week model-refresh cadence. I have a hypothesis about where that re-validation loop breaks first.

A 225-year frame

For 225 years, the firm has grown by deciding carefully which work belongs on which balance sheet. The same discipline applies to the AI program now underway across the house.

The public signals are clear. LLM Suite reached 230,000 employees by the end of 2025. The 2025 Annual Report positions AI alongside electricity and the internet as a generational shift. The $18 billion technology budget and the 63,000-person technology organization are the largest in banking. Four hundred and fifty use cases are in production today, and the firm has targeted a thousand by the end of 2026. The Miami Private Bank team on Brickell Avenue serves an international UHNW book that runs on cross-border compliance as much as on returns.

The governance-to-embedding gap

Three allocation questions sit inside every workflow.

  1. 01

    Which part of each workflow is best served by a large language model.

  2. 02

    Which part belongs in deterministic code, or in a rules engine inside the Model Risk Management perimeter.

  3. 03

    Which part still belongs with a licensed adviser. Getting that allocation right is the work. Getting it wrong is expensive both ways: models asked to do what a database should do, and databases asked to handle what only a trained reviewer should handle.

A working frame

Roughly how the problems allocate once you look inside them.

60%

Traditional code and database work

Problems that arrive wearing an AI label are, on inspection, a pipeline, a join, a scheduled job, or a report that should have been built in engineering.

30%

Rule-based logic

Properly-scoped decision systems handle these with guarantees the regulators already accept. SR 11-7 documentation maps cleanly onto deterministic logic.

10%

Genuine language, reasoning, generation

A model is the right tool. This is the compression point between eight-week LLM Suite refresh cycles and multi-month MRM validation.

The goal of a tight engagement is to move each workflow onto the layer where it costs the least to govern and delivers the most measurable productivity.
